Stephen Hawking saying that 'black holes are not that black after all' and that they emit 'Hawking radiation' A central law for black holes predicts that the total area of their event horizons – the boundary beyond which nothing can ever escape – should never shrink. This law is Hawking's area theorem, named after physicist Stephen Hawking, who derived the theorem in 1971.
